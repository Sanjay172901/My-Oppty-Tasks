What is Mutual fund

Published on November 09, 2025 by Kattappa

A mutual fund is a financial instrument that collects money from various investors to invest in a mix of assets such as stocks, bonds, and other securities. This pooling of resources allows investors to achieve diversification and professional management, which would be difficult to accomplish individually. When you invest in a mutual fund, you buy shares in the fund, becoming a partial owner of all its holdings.

How DO Mutual Funds Work??

Published on November 09, 2025 by Kattappa

Pooling of Money: Investors purchase shares or units of the mutual fund, contributing their money to a collective pool managed by professional fund managers.

2) Professional Management: Fund managers use the pooled money to buy a variety of assets according to the fund’s investment objectives and strategy. They research, select securities, and monitor performance.

3) Net Asset Value (NAV): The NAV represents the per-share value of the mutual fund, calculated by dividing the total value of the fund’s assets minus liabilities by the number of outstanding shares. NAV changes daily based on the performance of the fund’s investments.

4) Returns: Investors can earn returns through capital gains (when the value of the fund's assets increases), dividends, and interest distributions.
