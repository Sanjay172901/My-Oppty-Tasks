5. Headings & Paragraphs

Headings and paragraphs form the structural backbone of every HTML document. They define how content is organized, interpreted, and displayed by browsers and search engines. Understanding them deeply is essential for writing semantic and accessible HTML.

What Are Headings?

HTML provides six levels of headings: <h1> to <h6>. These are not just visual size differences — they define the hierarchical structure of a document. Search engines and screen readers rely on this hierarchy.

  • <h1> – Main topic of the page
  • <h2> – Major section
  • <h3> – Subsection
  • <h4> to <h6> – Deeper content levels

Example of Proper Heading Structure

<h1>Website Title</h1>

<h2>About Us</h2>
<h3>Our Mission</h3>

<h2>Services</h2>
<h3>Web Development</h3>

Output

Why Heading Hierarchy Matters

Improper heading order (like jumping from <h1> to <h4>) breaks document structure. This affects:

  • Search Engine Optimization (SEO)
  • Accessibility tools
  • Content readability

Headings should follow a logical, nested structure like an outline.

What Are Paragraphs?

The <p> tag defines a paragraph — a block of related text. Browsers automatically add vertical spacing before and after paragraphs. Paragraphs help separate ideas clearly.

Paragraph Example

<p>This is the first paragraph.</p>
<p>This is the second paragraph explaining another idea.</p>

Output

Paragraph vs Line Break

The <br> tag inserts a line break inside a paragraph. It does not create a new paragraph. 

<p>
Line One<br>
Line Two
</p>

Output

Best Practices

  • Use only one <h1> per page.
  • Maintain logical heading order.
  • Do not use headings only for styling.
  • Keep paragraphs concise and meaningful.
  • Use CSS for visual styling, not structural tags.

Real-World Structured Example

<h1>My Blog</h1>

<h2>Introduction</h2>
<p>Welcome to my blog where I share web development tips.</p>

<h2>Latest Post</h2>
<h3>Understanding HTML Basics</h3>
<p>HTML is the foundation of web development.</p>

Output
